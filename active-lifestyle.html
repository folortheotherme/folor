Benefits of Working out in Groups

  • 29 July 2018
  • by Tracy Cao
Working with a team to achieve physical fitness has been proven to be more productive than working alone.

A study discovered that ninety five percent of people who started a weight loss program with friends completed the program. It can be hard dragging your tired body out of bed or out of the house to go work out. Of course it is important to work out, but that doesn't change the difficulty that comes with motivating oneself to exercise. There is the need for a motivational park, as defined by NBC news, to help us achieve physical fitness. This holds even truer for women.

A recent study by Sport's England showed that women are fifty percent less likely to exercise regularly when compared to men. This however doesn't reflect the gender who gains more health wise from exercise. Women have higher quality of life due to exercise than men. While men exercised for the enjoyment, women exercised for weight loss and toning. The reason for the exercise was the major reason for the improved quality of life amongst the women.

Women who want to keep physically fit have subscribed to group exercising. While this has worked well for keeping physically fit, there are also a number of other advantages that come attached with group exercise.

Most women have an issue with exercising. The reasons for this has is not known in its totality, but there is a high possibility, according to Paul Loprinzi, an assistant professor of exercise science at Bellarmine University, lack of confidence is a major reason why women engage less in exercise.

This lack of confidence can be overcome by GROUP EXERCISE for women.

Benefits of group exercise

For women, physical exercise with a group of friends or or group of other women they do not have personal relationships with prior transcends the mere stretching and jogging and cat poses, it is serves as a social group that allows them network, connect and build meaningful relationships.

1. Group exercise helps improve mental health:

Researchers at Australia, using the women at the Australia Sports Club as case study, discovered that women who participated in the group sport activities at the club had better mental health than those women who exercised at the gym or walked alone. The research did not find any difference in the physical health of these women; instead there was a significant difference in the way and manner the different groups of women expressed gratitude.

Those women who worked in group were better able to express gratitude towards their team mates and this led to better relationships with the teammates and with themselves as well. These women who expressed gratitude to their friends felt more closer to their friends than the group who worked out alone.

2. Helps women build co-operation and interaction with other women:

Meeting people that share common interests is one sure way to build better relationships and network. Group exercise helps women create and build networks for work, career and business.

Let us take Kate for example.

Kate owns a media consulting company that helps businesses create useful content that drives sales and boosts brand equity. Over the past three months she had been trying to secure a contract with a Fortune 500 firm but has been unable to make headway. Every proposal she had sent was left unattended to - she never got a reply from them. She was beginning to get frustrated. In the health and fitness club she joined some months ago, she met a woman with whom she had become close to. They often talked about the exercises they did and how it was helping them. Over dinner one day, after they finished with their netball game, Kate opened up to her new friend about the issues she was having sealing a contract with a company. Kate was oblivious of the woman's position, speaking out from a place of frustration. The woman offered to help her speak to the manager in charge of contracts at the firm where she was a B-level manager. A few weeks later, Kate is presenting her content marketing plan to the head of departments at the firm. For women who have issues networking, group exercise at the gym is a sure way to build meaningful and useful relationship with other women.

3. It can help improve moods:

A study published in the Journal of American Osteopathic found that group exercise helped medical students improve their moods and deal with stress. The study participants completed a stress survey once every four weeks for twelve weeks. The result showed a reduction in stress levels of students who participated in group exercise when compared to those students who didn't participate in group exercise. Women go through tight schedules at home and work, and this leads to high level of stress. A survey by Pollstar and SurveyMonkey discovered that fifty eight percent of women get stressed out at work, which if not checked and nipped in the bud could lead to workplace depression and might deteriorate into other mental illnesses.

Group exercise gives women associations of shared and common struggles, thus helping them to relax and ease off the stress from the workplace and from the homes.

4. Helps women gets support in accountability:

Getting off work or out of bed or from behind the desk can be a difficult task for women. The busy schedule can make most postpone going to exercise, but with a group exercise, you are held accountable by other group members. Accountability helps us achieve our goals, and goals that have been achieved fuels personal confidence. Women who engage in group exercise and team sports are more compelled to complete tasks at the gym and more happy to. Task completion at the gym with a group of other women boosts self-confidence and translates to completion of other personal tasks.

Every woman who is interested in keeping fit, socializing and networking should not allow self-doubt stop them from enjoying the enormous benefits that comes with exercising, not to mention the endless stream of opportunities and emotional buffers that exercise groups and team sports provides. Whether it is basketball, or netball, or even just a team of women jogging every weekend as a group, it is beneficial to your physical, mental and social health. Take advantage of group exercise and meet other women who are staying physically healthy while they build productive relationships.
